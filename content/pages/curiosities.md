Not everything needs a grant. These are the side-quests — made out of curiosity, taught me
something, and are too much fun to leave off the record.

## Deciphering Beanish

Thirty-five utterances of a language that exists only in an [xkcd](https://xkcd.com)
comic — *Beanish*, from Randall Munroe's "Time." I mapped its script to Canadian Aboriginal
Syllabics and worked backward from a character's broken English to reconstruct a grammar.
Statistical methods built for millions of words, applied to a corpus you could fit on a
napkin. It even helped me land a post-doc, once.

[**github.com/tresoldi/beanish**](https://github.com/tresoldi/beanish) · an essay is on its way

## Runestaves, visualised

An interactive visualisation of the evolution of **runestaves** — the kind of ambiguous,
high-cardinality glyph data that started me down the [row2vec](https://github.com/evotext/row2vec)
road, and part of the broader [cultural-evolution-of-texts](/research/) work.

[**github.com/tresoldi/runestaves_viz**](https://github.com/tresoldi/runestaves_viz)

## A Bloom filter in COBOL

Because someone had to. A probabilistic set-membership structure implemented in **COBOL** —

[**github.com/tresoldi/cobol_bloom**](https://github.com/tresoldi/cobol_bloom)

## Tafl games

The ancient Nordic family of asymmetric board games, approached with chess-programming
techniques — minimax, evaluation functions, and the peculiar problem of a game where the two
sides have entirely different goals. A recurring sport.
