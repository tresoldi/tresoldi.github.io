My research is in computational historical linguistics, with a particular emphasis on
native South American languages and on phylogenetic methods that take phonology
seriously.

## Current projects

**Arca Verborum** — a large-scale, analysis-ready lexical database for computational
historical linguistics. [Project page](https://www.tresoldi.org/arcaverborum).

**PhyloVector** — vector-based approaches to phylogenetic analysis in historical
linguistics.

**Uralic phylogeny** — computational methods for relationships within the Uralic family.

**Arawan family** — a Bayesian phylogenetic study of the Arawan languages of South America
(in preparation).

**Large language models in production** — practical work with LLMs, via APIs and locally
deployed, for linguistic analysis and beyond.

## Philosophy

I care about **open science** (data and methods in the open), **reproducibility**
(results that others can verify), **collaboration** across disciplines, and
**community engagement** with the language communities whose histories this work touches.
